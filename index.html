[ PHILOSOPHY ]

Being an Impartial is a way of being that can appear strange or incomprehensible to many people.


Nevertheless, an Impartial is a strong believer in rational analysis of situations. Many people confuse it as a lack of convictions or disinterest in taking a stance. On the contrary, an impartial follows clear and defined principles, striving to apply them in a balanced way, without definitive and clear alignments in most situations.


Usually the goal of many impartials is to promote dialogue, mutual understanding, and peaceful conflict resolution. This approach does not stem from indecision or weakness, but from the conviction that it is the best way to face life's situations.


However, this attitude is often misunderstood by giving the impression of detachment or apathy. In reality, there is strong effort to maintain calm and analyze situations from multiple perspectives before acting or taking any position. It is believed that the truth rarely lies on just one side and that the best solutions emerge from confrontation and the ability to listen to others.


// Impartials have many strengths:


Fairness: Impartial individuals treat everyone equally, ensuring that decisions are made based on merit rather than personal biases or relationships.


Objectivity: They are honest and able to assess situations and information without being influenced by emotions or preconceived notions, leading to more rational and sound conclusions.


Conflict resolution: Impartiality fosters trust and respect, enabling them to mediate conflicts and find common ground among differing parties or opinions.


Encouragement of open dialogue: Their neutrality can create an environment where diverse perspectives can be shared and discussed openly, promoting collaboration, understanding and peace.


Better decision-making: Impartiality allows for a comprehensive evaluation of as many possible of all relevant factors, leading to more balanced decisions.


Promotion of equality: An impartial approach helps to dismantle systemic biases and promotes equality, contributing to a fair society.


Friendliness: Most impartial individuals are genuinely nice and friendly to everyone. It is very unlikely that they will ever start an argument or a fight.


Helpful: Offering help in difficult situations is often essential for them. Their genuine approach to problem-solving creates a strong bond with people in need. This is usually how they show their empathy.


I wish people could truly understand this way of being, which is not indifference, but a sincere desire to create harmony and avoid unnecessary conflicts.
